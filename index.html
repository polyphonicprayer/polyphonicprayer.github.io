About me

My name is Michał Sroka and I'm a 25-year-old
Game Designer at Solid9 Studio.

You can contact me at michal@sroka.me,
+48 793 278 977 or via LinkedIn.

I graduated English Studies spec. Design of Interactive Entertainment and Localization of Games and Software from University of Silesia.

Even though I've been an avid gamer my whole life, the aforementioned studies served as a formal stepping stone to the game design career I've always dreamt of. During my time at the University my love for retro gaming led me to becoming a volunteer tour guide at the Museum of the History of Computers and Information Technology and a translator for Komoda&Amiga Plus Magazine.

After graduation I landed my first job in the gaming industry at Solid9 Studio and in a short while I was put in a position of the lead designer of Exorder. Such a responsibility allowed me to quickly develop new skills and enabled me to oversee various parts of the game production process.

I remain open to experiencing new challenges and opportunities in my career.

Commercial projects

Game Designer
Solid9 Studio
(June 2017 - present)

I was hired at Solid9 Studio to design Exorder from the ground up. Being the only designer in the company enabled me to take on diverse responsibilities, such as:

  • gameplay design - designing all mechanics and ensuring that the vision of the game stays consistent throughout the entire production process,
  • level design - designing and creating all levels for the game in the Unity engine,
  • narrative design and translation - story, dialogues and the entirety of in-game text in both English and Polish,
  • documentation - creating and maintaining GDD and other necessary documents,
  • UI/UX design - designing the UI and creating mockups in Photoshop,
  • minor graphics creation - creating minor graphical assets for the game (e.g. achievement icons),
  • marketing materials creation - creating and editing adverts, trailers and social media content.


Translator
K&A Plus Magazine
(November 2015 - present)

Since late 2015 I've been a part of Komoda & Amiga Plus Magazine editorial team. My work consists mostly of translating, but I've also been a general advisor for the magazine.

Other

LDS (hobby project)

A complete UE4 level with very simple gameplay mechanics. The goal of the player is to search for busts of David. After a while the Biblical hero himself appears (in the form of even bigger bust) and chases the player to the exit. The game uses ready-made assets found on the Internet. The level is heavily inspired by LSD: Dream Emulator and general Vaporwave aesthetic.


Broken VR (student project)

A complete UE4 level with implemented gameplay mechanics. The goal of the player is to find keys scattered around the level in order to open the exit. The game uses ready-made assets found on the Internet. The level was supposed to be a contribution to a university group project.


Basic 3D models

I've also been doing some basic low-poly modelling in Autodesk Maya. As an avid PS1 gamer I'm really fond of the low-poly flat-shaded aesthetic.
