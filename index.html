About me

My name is Michał Sroka and I'm a 25-year-old
Game Designer at Solid9 Studio.

You can contact me at michal@sroka.me,
+48 793 278 977 or via LinkedIn.

I graduated English Studies spec. Design of Interactive Entertainment and Localization of Games and Software from University of Silesia.

Even though I've been an avid gamer my whole life, the aforementioned studies served as a formal stepping stone to the game design career I've always dreamt of.

During my time at the University my love for retro gaming led me to becoming a volunteer tour guide at the Museum of the History of Computers and Information Technology and a translator for Komoda&Amiga Plus Magazine.

After graduation I landed my first job in the gaming industry at Solid9 Studio and in a short while I was put in a position of the designer of Exorder. Such a responsibility allowed me to quickly develop new skills and enabled me to oversee various parts of the game production process.

I remain open to experiencing new challenges and opportunities in my career.

Commercial projects

Game Designer
Solid9 Studio
(June 2017 - present)

I was hired at Solid9 Studio to design Exorder (PC, Switch, Xbox One, mobiles) from the ground up. Being the only designer in the company enabled me to take on diverse responsibilities, such as:

  • gameplay design - designing all the mechanics and ensuring that the vision of the game stays consistent throughout the entire production process,
  • level design and scripting - designing, scripting and meshing all the levels for the game in the Unity engine (12 Campaign levels, 8 Challenges and 8 Skirmish maps),
  • narrative design and translation - story, dialogues and the entirety of in-game text in both English and Polish,
  • documentation - creating and maintaining GDD and other necessary documents,
  • UI/UX design - designing the UI and creating mockups in Photoshop,
  • minor graphics creation - creating minor graphical assets for the game (e.g. achievement icons), and editing textures (small alterations and palette swaps),
  • marketing materials creation - creating and editing adverts, trailers and social media content.


Translator

Other

LDS (hobby project)

A complete UE4 level with very simple gameplay mechanics. The goal of the player is to search for busts of David. After a while the Biblical hero himself appears (in the form of even bigger bust) and chases the player to the exit. The game uses ready-made assets found on the Internet. The level is heavily inspired by LSD: Dream Emulator and general Vaporwave aesthetic.


Broken VR (student project)

A complete UE4 level with implemented gameplay mechanics. The goal of the player is to find keys scattered around the level in order to open the exit. The game uses ready-made assets found on the Internet. The level was supposed to be a contribution to a university group project.


2D graphic

Graphic design can become quite handy when working in a small indie studio. Beside helping me get my point across in various game design documents, I've used my skills to create:

  • all achievement icons - doing renders in Unity and turning them into icons,
  • alterations to textures and materials in Exorder - taking textures and modifying them, along with their respective materials, to create variations of already existing units,
  • promotional materials - designing and creating promo screenshots, adverts, GIFs and social media content,
  • UI mockups - by creating new placeholder designs or by modyfing already existing elements,
