Broken VR

A complete UE4 level with implemented gameplay mechanics. The goal of the player is to find keys scattered around the level in order to open the exit. The game uses ready-made assets found on the Internet. The level was supposed to be a contribution to a group project.

LDS

A complete UE4 level with very simple gameplay mechanics. The goal of the player is to search for busts of David. After a while the Biblical hero himself appears in the form of even bigger bust and chases the player to the exit. The game uses ready-made assets found on the Internet. The level is heavily inspired by LSD: Dream Emulator and general Vaporwave aesthetic.

Whiteboxed FPS level

A complete whiteboxed UE4 level. Though there is no gameplay mechanics, nor proper assets implemented, the level can be considered complete from the design perspective.
The level is inspired by The Darkness II and Half Life 2.

Cel-shaded FPS platformer concept

A concept FPS level with partially implemented gameplay mechanics. The goal of the player is to complete the obstacle course. By shooting special cubes, the player opens doors and scores points. Background music reacts to the score - with each cube shot a new musical track plays, ultimately forming the whole song (first the general rhythm track, then bass, drums etc.).

Basic 3D modelling

I've also been doing some basic low-poly modelling in Autodesk Maya.
As an avid PS1 gamer I'm really fond of the low-poly flat-shaded aesthetic.


K&A Plus

Since late 2015 I've been a part of Komoda & Amiga Plus Magazine editorial team.
My work consists mostly of translating, but I've also been a general advisor for the magazine.


BA Dissertation

My Bachelor dissertation regarded the topic of rhythm game controllers. The full title was: "Controller as a Performance Instrument. An Analysis of the Evolution of Rhythm Games Controllers".
