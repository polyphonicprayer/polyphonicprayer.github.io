About me

My name is Michał Sroka and I'm 24 years old. I'm currently working as a Game Designer at Solid9 Studio.

I graduated English Studies spec. Design of Interactive Entertainment and Localization of Games and Software from University of Silesia.

My interests revolve around video games, music and foreign languages.

My beloved video game genre is rhythm games with the all-time favourite title being Parappa The Rapper.

When it comes to music, I'm obsessed with the works of Damon Albarn and I consider Gorillaz to be my favourite musical project. It inspired me to take up playing drums as a hobby.

As for the languages, I'm a native speaker of Polish. On top of that, I'm fluent in English and speak Norwegian on an intermediate level. I also know the basics of German.

Commercial projects

Solid9: Game Designer (June 2017 - present)

I was hired by Solid9 Studio to design Exorder from the ground up. Being the only designer in the company (out of three people total) enabled me to take on diverse responsibilities, such as:

  • gameplay mechanics design - designing mechanics and ensuring that the vision of the game stays consistent throughout the entire production process,
  • narrative design - story, dialogues and the entirety of in-game text,
  • level design - designing and creating all levels in the game in the Unity engine,
  • documentation - creating and maintaining GDD and other necessary documents,
  • UI/UX design - designing the UI and creating mockups in Photoshop,
  • game balance - balancing the game and working with testers,
  • marketing materials creation - editing and creating adverts, trailers and social media content.


K&A Plus: Translator (November 2015 - present)

Since late 2015 I've been a part of Komoda & Amiga Plus Magazine editorial team. My work consists mostly of translating, but I've also been a general advisor for the magazine.

Other

LDS (hobby project)

A complete UE4 level with very simple gameplay mechanics. The goal of the player is to search for busts of David. After a while the Biblical hero himself appears (in the form of even bigger bust) and chases the player to the exit. The game uses ready-made assets found on the Internet. The level is heavily inspired by LSD: Dream Emulator and general Vaporwave aesthetic.


Broken VR (student project)

A complete UE4 level with implemented gameplay mechanics. The goal of the player is to find keys scattered around the level in order to open the exit. The game uses ready-made assets found on the Internet. The level was supposed to be a contribution to a university group project.


Basic 3D models

I've also been doing some basic low-poly modelling in Autodesk Maya. As an avid PS1 gamer I'm really fond of the low-poly flat-shaded aesthetic.
